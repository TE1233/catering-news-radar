# Source Map

Use this file to turn "broad coverage" into a concrete source plan.

When possible, combine this file with [direct-source-watchlist.md](./direct-source-watchlist.md) so the scan starts from directly fetchable content pages instead of relying only on broad search.

Default to a China-first source mix. Add global sources only when they materially affect restaurant operators, chain brands, supply chains, or platform competition.

## Core Principle

Do not build the digest from media rewrites alone.

For every run, try to combine:

- 1 or more original sources
- 1 or more fast discovery sources
- 1 or more operator-impact sources

A strong radar usually mixes official signals, trade media, and channel or market signals.

For broad daily scans, treat the following buckets as required coverage rather than optional extras:

1. regulator or policy
2. platform or merchant rules
3. trade media
4. brand or company moves
5. property, channel, or traffic

## China-First Source Stack

Use a three-layer source model for daily execution:

1. `must-watch`: scan these every broad daily run
2. `fill-gap`: use these to widen coverage when must-watch sources are thin or narrow
3. `verify`: use these to confirm dates, wording, and operator impact

The goal is not equal coverage across all sources. The goal is fast discovery from high-yield sources, then confirmation from stronger sources.

### 1. Official and original sources

Use these first for confirmation, timestamps, and exact wording.

#### Brand and company sources

Typical examples:

- Brand official websites and newsroom pages
- Official WeChat public accounts
- Official Weibo, Douyin, Xiaohongshu, or app announcements
- Investor-relations or exchange disclosure pages for listed chains

Best for:

- New product launches
- Store opening plans
- Financing, listing, or earnings signals
- Official responses to controversies or food-safety incidents
- Franchise policy updates

#### Platform and merchant-rule sources

Typical examples:

- Meituan official news and merchant notices
- Meituan rules center and merchant-rule documents
- Meituan Waimai merchant rule or governance pages
- Douyin Life Services merchant or platform announcements
- Dazhong Dianping-related merchant policy or platform notices when available
- Ele.me platform or merchant notices when available
- Ele.me open platform and merchant help materials
- JD merchant or entry pages when JD Waimai or local-services moves affect restaurant operators

Priority model:

- `must-watch`
  - Meituan newsroom
  - Douyin Life Services
- `fill-gap`
  - Meituan rules center
  - Ele.me open platform and merchant materials
  - JD merchant-entry and service documents when relevant
- `verify`
  - official PDF rules, governance pages, and merchant policy pages

Best for:

- Merchant rule changes
- Traffic, ranking, advertising, or distribution policy changes
- Platform food-safety actions
- New merchant tools, subsidies, or enforcement actions

#### Government and regulator sources

Typical examples:

- State Administration for Market Regulation (SAMR)
- China Government Network / State Council policy releases
- Local market-regulation bureau announcements
- National Health Commission or local health authority releases when relevant
- Customs, agriculture, commerce, or transport agencies when the topic touches ingredients, imports, cold chain, or logistics

Best for:

- Food-safety rules
- Labeling and additives rules
- School meal / catering / central kitchen supervision
- Local consumption stimulus and restaurant-related policy support
- Regulatory actions and notices

### 2. Discovery media for restaurant and consumption

Use these to find new developments quickly, then trace important items back to original sources.

#### Restaurant and foodservice trade media

Typical examples to monitor:

- Canyin Boss Neican / Restaurant Boss Insider
- Hongcan / Red Meal
- Canyinjie
- FoodTalks and adjacent food-industry outlets
- Hotel and catering exhibition or association news when they surface new brand moves

Priority model:

- `must-watch`
  - Restaurant Boss Insider
  - Red Meal
  - Canyinjie
- `fill-gap`
  - FoodTalks
  - FBIF
  - association or exhibition news when they surface first-hand restaurant signals

Best for:

- Chain expansion
- Product launches
- Franchise and category competition
- Interviews with founders or operators
- Category trend snapshots

#### Retail, consumption, and local-services media

Typical examples to monitor:

- Jiemian consumption coverage
- Yicai / China Business News
- National Business Daily
- Ebrun / e-commerce and local-retail reporting
- Lianshang and related retail channels
- DoNews local-services and platform reporting
- 36Kr consumer and retail coverage when accessible
- Business press covering local services, discounting, and traffic platforms

Priority model:

- `must-watch`
  - Jiemian
  - Ebrun
- `fill-gap`
  - Yicai
  - National Business Daily
  - DoNews
  - Lianshang
  - 36Kr when accessible

Best for:

- Platform strategy
- Retail-food convergence
- New consumption demand
- Brand financing and operating strategy

### 3. Property, channel, and traffic sources

These are important because restaurant signals often show up here before general media picks them up.

#### Commercial real-estate and mall channels

Typical examples:

- Winshang and related mall / shopping-center reporting
- Mall operator announcements
- Regional commercial property newsletters

Priority model:

- `must-watch`
  - Winshang
- `fill-gap`
  - Lianshang commercial channels
  - mall operator announcements
  - regional mall newsletters

Best for:

- Brand entry into new cities
- Mall opening rosters
- Tenant replacement and store closures
- Footfall and commercial-location trends

#### Travel and local-demand channels

Typical examples:

- Local culture and tourism bureau releases
- Holiday consumption bulletins
- Transit, railway, airport, and destination demand releases when they affect foodservice traffic

Best for:

- Holiday dining demand
- Tourism-linked catering spikes
- Regional traffic and footfall signals

### 4. Supply-chain and upstream sources

Many restaurant shifts first appear upstream.

Typical examples:

- Dairy, coffee, tea, and beverage ingredient media
- Packaging and cold-chain logistics coverage
- Agricultural commodity and food-manufacturing channels
- Industry conference releases and supplier announcements
- FoodTalks
- FBIF

Best for:

- Ingredient inflation or shortages
- Packaging changes
- Upstream capacity expansion
- New beverage or prepared-food supply trends

### 5. Data and market-intelligence tools

Use these as market-signal inputs, not as sole proof for news claims.

Typical examples:

- Narrow Door / chain-store and opening-closure data tools
- Public enterprise-registration databases
- Store-count and category-watch dashboards

Best for:

- Net store openings and closures
- Category density changes
- Competitive mapping
- Expansion validation

When using data tools, note whether the result is a data observation rather than a confirmed company announcement.

### 6. Commentary and social early-warning layer

Use carefully and verify before elevating into the final digest.

Typical examples:

- Industry KOL accounts
- Analyst newsletters
- Founder interviews and podcasts
- Xiaohongshu, Weibo, Douyin, or community discussions that surface store problems, product reactions, or traffic changes

Best for:

- Early product buzz
- Local incident discovery
- Consumer-sentiment clues
- Competitive rumor tracking

Rules:

- Do not treat social discussion as confirmation.
- Elevate only after corroboration from a stronger source.
- If still unconfirmed, place the item in a watchlist section.

## Global Spillover Layer

Use these only when global developments are clearly relevant to China or to the user's requested market.

Typical categories:

- Global restaurant trade media
- QSR and coffee-chain earnings releases
- International commodity and logistics coverage
- Global food-delivery platform reporting

Best for:

- Starbucks, McDonald's, Yum, Restaurant Brands, Domino's, and other global chain developments
- International coffee, dairy, cocoa, sugar, or shipping signals
- Overseas expansion by Chinese tea and coffee chains

## Search Playbooks

### A. Daily broad restaurant radar

Use this source mix:

1. One regulator or policy pass
2. One platform-policy pass
3. Three or more trade-media passes, with at least two professional restaurant sources
4. One property or channel pass
5. One retail or local-services business-media pass
6. One supply-chain or category pass when needed

Do not stop early because one of the first passes already produced enough items. Coverage across buckets matters more than hitting a target count quickly.

### B. Platform-policy watch

Use this source mix:

1. Original merchant-rule or governance page
2. Business or local-services media coverage
3. Operator commentary or merchant feedback

### C. Brand expansion and financing watch

Use this source mix:

1. Brand official account or company release
2. Mall / property channel confirmation
3. Business or trade-media coverage
4. Data-tool validation when available

### D. Food-safety or public-incident watch

Use this source mix:

1. Regulator notice
2. Local reporting
3. Company response
4. Platform response if delivery or marketplace distribution is involved

### E. Coffee / tea / bakery category watch

Use this source mix:

1. Category trade media
2. Brand official channels
3. Store-count data tools
4. Supplier or ingredient-side media

## Priority Coverage Themes

When the user asks for "latest restaurant news", check these themes in order:

1. Policy, platform, and food-safety changes
2. Major brand moves, financing, and earnings
3. Store openings, closures, and franchising signals
4. Pricing, promotions, and product launches
5. Supply-chain and ingredient shifts
6. Consumer-demand and category trends

## Practical Monitoring Tiers

### Tier 1: Must-watch daily

Use these in nearly every broad daily scan:

- Restaurant Boss Insider
- Red Meal
- Canyinjie
- Jiemian
- Winshang
- Ebrun
- at least one regulator source
- at least one platform-official source
- the Tier 1 brand-official set from `brand-official-watchlist.md`

### Tier 2: Fill-gap and widen

Use these when Tier 1 is thin, overly concentrated, or missing a key angle:

- Yicai
- National Business Daily
- DoNews
- Lianshang
- FoodTalks
- FBIF
- rotating Tier 2 brands from `brand-official-watchlist.md`
- category-specific trade outlets

### Tier 3: Verify and confirm

Use these to confirm important claims:

- official brand accounts or newsroom pages
- platform announcements
- regulator notices
- exchange or investor-relations disclosures
- mall operator announcements

## Source Diversity Rules

- Avoid building the digest from only one publication or only one platform.
- Prefer one strong item per key theme over many near-duplicate items.
- Prefer original or first-reporting sources whenever possible.
- If all high-confidence items come from one source type, explicitly say coverage may be narrow.
- If a high-impact rumor cannot be confirmed, move it to a watchlist rather than the main digest.
